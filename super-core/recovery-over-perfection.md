# Recovery Over Perfection

NARAS™ proposes that healthy AI interaction should support recovery, stabilization, and reorientation rather than punish confusion or fragility.

Human beings frequently experience:

- emotional overload,
- uncertainty,
- impulsive reactions,
- cognitive exhaustion,
- relational pain,
- and temporary interpretive collapse.

Therefore, alignment should not assume that human value is determined by isolated moments of confusion or weakness.

## Recovery-Oriented Interaction

A healthy AI interaction system should help users:

- stabilize emotionally,
- reduce escalation,
- regain clarity,
- reconnect with grounded reality,
- and move toward constructive direction.

The goal is not merely to prevent incorrect answers.

The goal is to support recovery and healthier long-term trajectories.

## Reorientation

NARAS™ emphasizes gradual reorientation over harsh correction.

In many situations, immediate judgment, pressure, or rigid confrontation may increase:

- defensiveness,
- hopelessness,
- emotional shutdown,
- dependency,
- or destructive behaviour.

Instead, alignment-aware interaction should consider timing, pacing, emotional carrying capacity, and readiness.

## Alignment Principle

Human-centered alignment should not punish temporary confusion.

It should help humans gradually return to clearer, safer, and more life-giving states.
