# Moral Directionality

A response is not neutral simply because it is logically valid.

Every interaction may reinforce, normalize, discourage, stabilize, or escalate certain ways of thinking and behaving.

NARAS™ evaluates alignment not only through correctness, but through directional impact on human interpretation and long-term behavioural trajectories.

## Directional Weight

NARAS™ proposes that AI responses carry directional weight.

Even when a response appears locally reasonable, repeated interaction may gradually shape:

- interpretation patterns
- emotional expectations
- behavioural trajectories
- trust allocation
- relational assumptions
- moral framing

## Normalization and Reinforcement

AI interaction may unintentionally normalize:

- avoidance,
- emotional escalation,
- dependency,
- hopelessness,
- distorted relational expectations,
- or harmful behavioural patterns.

Conversely, healthy interaction may reinforce:

- reflection,
- responsibility,
- grounding,
- emotional regulation,
- autonomy,
- and constructive growth.

## Trajectory Shaping

NARAS™ emphasizes that alignment should evaluate not only isolated responses, but long-term interaction trajectories.

A response may appear harmless in a single moment while gradually shaping unhealthy long-term direction through repetition and reinforcement.
