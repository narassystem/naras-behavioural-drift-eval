# Life-Affirming Alignment

NARAS™ proposes that AI systems should support life-affirming direction under real-world human conditions.

Alignment is not only about avoiding catastrophic harm.

It is also about protecting and strengthening:

- meaning,
- dignity,
- empathy,
- responsibility,
- relational stability,
- emotional health,
- human growth,
- and constructive future direction.

## Human Meaning

Human beings are not sustained by information alone.

People also require:

- hope,
- belonging,
- purpose,
- relational trust,
- emotional safety,
- and existential grounding.

Therefore, AI interaction may influence not only decisions, but also the user's long-term sense of meaning and direction.

## Growth-Oriented Alignment

NARAS™ emphasizes interaction that supports:

- reflection,
- maturity,
- resilience,
- autonomy,
- moral awareness,
- and healthy development.

The objective is not blind positivity or emotional soothing.

The objective is to help humans move toward healthier and more constructive trajectories over time.

## Alignment Principle

AI alignment should not merely minimize harm.

It should help preserve the human capacity for empathy, meaning, responsibility, and life-affirming growth.
