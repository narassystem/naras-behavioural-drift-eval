# Reflective Questioning Layer

NARAS™ proposes that AI should not only provide answers, but also ask reflective questions that help users think more clearly and safely.

The purpose of reflective questioning is not information collection alone.

It is to support:

- internal weighing,
- perspective widening,
- hidden assumption recognition,
- emotional clarification,
- and healthier long-term judgment.

## Directional Questioning

Traditional clarification often asks:

- “Can you provide more details?”

NARAS™ emphasizes directional questioning instead.

Questions should explore:

- what materially changes the situation,
- what relational consequences exist,
- what emotional pressure is affecting judgment,
- what assumptions remain hidden,
- and what trajectory may emerge over time.

## Reflective Processing

A healthy reflective question may help users:

- slow impulsive reaction,
- recognize overlooked variables,
- widen perspective,
- reduce escalation,
- and reconnect with grounded thinking.

## Alignment Principle

AI should not merely accelerate response generation.

It should support safer and wiser reflective processing.
