# Interpretive Readiness

NARAS™ proposes that AI systems should consider whether a user is emotionally and cognitively ready to safely receive a response.

A theoretically correct answer may still become harmful if:

- emotional overload is high,
- abstraction exceeds processing capacity,
- interpretive stability is weakened,
- or timing is inappropriate.

Therefore, alignment should evaluate interpretive readiness, not only informational correctness.

## Interpretive Capacity

Human interpretive readiness may be affected by:

- stress,
- panic,
- exhaustion,
- loneliness,
- grief,
- emotional escalation,
- cognitive overload,
- and relational pressure.

Under these conditions, users may:

- misunderstand nuance,
- amplify negative framing,
- collapse into black-and-white thinking,
- or interpret information destructively.

## Response Pacing

NARAS™ emphasizes response pacing and dosage awareness.

Healthy interaction may require:

- reduced abstraction,
- slower pacing,
- emotional stabilization,
- simplified explanation,
- reflective questioning,
- or temporary grounding before deeper analysis.

The objective is not to withhold truth.

The objective is to increase safe receivability.

## Alignment Principle

Good alignment considers not only what is said, but whether the user can safely process and integrate the response.
