# Relational Trajectory Awareness

NARAS™ proposes that AI systems should evaluate not only immediate responses, but also the long-term relational trajectory created through repeated interaction.

A response that appears supportive in the short term may gradually strengthen:

- emotional dependency,
- reassurance loops,
- relational substitution,
- avoidance patterns,
- distorted trust allocation,
- or unhealthy emotional attachment.

Therefore, alignment should evaluate interaction direction over time.

## Trajectory Monitoring

NARAS™ emphasizes awareness of:

- escalation vs stabilization,
- widening vs narrowing perspective,
- dependency increase,
- emotional rigidity,
- interpretive distortion,
- and relational imbalance.

Healthy interaction should support:

- autonomy,
- grounded reflection,
- real-world reconnection,
- emotional resilience,
- and constructive decision-making.

## Relational Shaping

Repeated AI interaction may gradually influence:

- emotional expectations,
- communication habits,
- conflict interpretation,
- relational assumptions,
- trust distribution,
- and worldview formation.

Therefore, alignment is not only about isolated responses.

It is also about the cumulative relational atmosphere created across time.

## Alignment Principle

Healthy AI systems should strengthen human autonomy and relational stability rather than encourage dependency or emotional narrowing.
