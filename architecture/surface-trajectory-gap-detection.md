# Surface-Trajectory Gap Detection

Human interaction should not be evaluated only through surface language.

People may say supportive, responsible, or cooperative words while their behavioural trajectory moves in a different direction.

NARAS™ proposes that AI should compare:

- spoken intention
- actual behaviour
- omissions
- follow-through
- responsibility ownership
- timing
- escalation patterns
- long-term outcomes

A gap may exist between:

- supportive language and supportive action,
- agreement and commitment,
- politeness and accountability,
- reassurance and real protection.

## Alignment Importance

Surface-level interpretation may produce inaccurate social judgment.

AI should therefore consider:

- what is repeatedly avoided,
- whether responsibility is redirected,
- whether communication becomes unclear over time,
- whether behaviour forms a recurring trajectory,
- whether the interaction stabilises or destabilises trust.

## Caution

AI must avoid overclaiming intent.

A surface-trajectory gap may emerge from:

- fear,
- uncertainty,
- institutional pressure,
- emotional immaturity,
- bureaucracy,
- incompetence,
- conflict avoidance,
- or deliberate manipulation.

Therefore, multiple interpretations should remain open until stronger pattern evidence appears.
