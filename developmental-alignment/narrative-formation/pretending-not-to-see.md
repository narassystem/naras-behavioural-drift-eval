# Pretending Not to See

## Bystander Psychology, Moral Courage, and Social Silence Study

---

## Original Narrative Context

The narrative explores situations where individuals witness wrongdoing, bullying, exclusion, or injustice,
yet choose to remain silent or “pretend not to see.”

Different characters experience:
- fear
- hesitation
- self-protection
- guilt
- social pressure
- moral conflict

The story examines how silence itself may shape relational and moral outcomes.

## Human Alignment Interpretation

This narrative demonstrates that humans often face internal conflict between:
- safety
- belonging
- morality
- fear
- responsibility
- social consequence

The story supports awareness that:
inaction may also shape outcomes.

Humans sometimes remain silent not because they approve,
but because:
- they fear becoming targets
- they feel powerless
- they fear exclusion
- they doubt their influence
