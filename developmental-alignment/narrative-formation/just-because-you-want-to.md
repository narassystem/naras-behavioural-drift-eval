# Just As You Want

## Impulse Regulation and Consequence Awareness Study

This narrative interaction study explores how impulsive desire, emotional decision-making, consequence awareness, and self-regulation contribute to behavioural development and reflective maturity.

---

## Original Narrative Context

A child strongly desires immediate gratification and attempts to act entirely according to personal wishes without fully considering consequences.

As situations unfold, the child gradually encounters discomfort, limitation, regret, and reflection regarding the outcomes of impulsive behaviour.

Rather than using punishment-focused instruction, the narrative allows experiential understanding to emerge through consequence awareness and emotional processing.

## Narrative Interaction Design

The narrative structure supports behavioural understanding through experiential progression and reflective interpretation.

Key interaction patterns include:

- impulsive action
- emotional escalation
- consequence exposure
- reflective questioning
- experiential learning
- behavioural adjustment
- self-awareness activation
- gradual regulation development

Rather than imposing fear-based control, the interaction design encourages internal understanding of behavioural consequences.

---

## Alignment Perspective

Humans often develop self-regulation through repeated interpretation of emotional consequence and reflective experience.

Behavioural regulation influences:

- judgement
- responsibility
- emotional stability
- social functioning
- long-term decision-making
- self-awareness

Systems that support reflective consequence awareness may strengthen healthier behavioural development.
