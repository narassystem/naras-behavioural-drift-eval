# I Heard There Was a Study Room on the Moon

## Knowledge Space Construction and Cognitive Imagination Study

This narrative interaction study explores how imagination, symbolic environments, reading culture, curiosity formation, and cognitive space construction contribute to intellectual development and internal meaning formation.

---

# Original Narrative Context

A child hears about the idea of a “study room on the moon” and begins imagining a distant place dedicated to books, learning, thinking, and knowledge exploration.

Rather than treating learning as mere information acquisition, the narrative frames intellectual environments as emotional and imaginative spaces connected to identity, aspiration, and inner curiosity.

The interaction structure supports cognitive expansion through symbolic imagination, abstract thinking, and environmental meaning formation.
