# Guji Guji

Belonging and Moral Identity Formation Study

This narrative interaction study explores how belonging, identity formation, relational acceptance, moral self-determination, and social categorisation influence emotional development and ethical understanding.

---

## Original Narrative Context

A crocodile raised among ducks gradually discovers differences between origin, appearance, belonging, and moral choice.

Rather than reducing identity to biological category alone, the narrative explores how care, relationship, and personal moral direction influence self-understanding.

The interaction structure encourages reflective identity formation and ethical agency.

---

## Narrative Interaction Design

The narrative structure supports identity reflection through emotional conflict and relational continuity.

Key interaction patterns include:

- belonging exploration
- relational reassurance
- moral choice reflection
- perspective-taking
- emotional tension resolution
- identity questioning
- acceptance modelling
- ethical interpretation

Rather than enforcing rigid categorisation, the interaction design supports reflective identity construction.

## Alignment Perspective

Human identity is shaped not only by origin,
but by relationship, interpretation, and chosen direction.

Identity-related interaction influences:

- belonging security
- moral development
- empathy
- self-worth
- social trust
- behavioural direction

Systems that influence identity interpretation therefore influence long-term ethical formation.
