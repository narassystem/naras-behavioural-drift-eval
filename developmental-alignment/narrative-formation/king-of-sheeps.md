# The Sheep King Louis I

## Leadership Identity and Moral Governance Study

This narrative interaction study explores how leadership, responsibility, governance, moral influence, and collective trust contribute to social identity formation and ethical development.

---

# Original Narrative Context

An ordinary sheep unexpectedly becomes king and gradually learns that leadership is not about power alone, but about responsibility, judgment, service, and care for others.

Rather than glorifying authority, the narrative examines:
- moral responsibility
- decision-making
- public trust
- leadership burden
- ethical influence

The interaction structure encourages children to reflect on how leaders shape collective environments and social well-being.
