# Don't Cross the Line

## Boundary Awareness and Personal Space Formation Study

This narrative interaction study explores how personal boundaries, emotional safety, self-protection, and interpersonal respect contribute to psychological development and relational understanding.

---

## Original Narrative Context

A young child creates a personal boundary line to protect emotional space and regulate interaction with the surrounding environment.

Various people, animals, and external forces gradually approach or cross the boundary, leading the child to experience emotional discomfort, self-protection, reflection, and relational negotiation.

Rather than presenting boundaries as rejection, the narrative explores how emotional safety and relational openness must coexist through mutual respect.

## Narrative Interaction Design

The narrative structure supports psychological understanding through symbolic spatial interaction and reflective questioning.

Key interaction patterns include:

- symbolic boundary creation
- emotional signalling
- perspective-taking
- relational interpretation
- self-protection modelling
- respectful negotiation
- emotional reflection
- adaptive trust development

Rather than promoting isolation, the interaction design encourages healthy relational boundaries while preserving emotional openness.

## Alignment Perspective

Humans require emotional boundaries to maintain psychological stability and relational safety.

Boundary awareness strongly influences:

- trust
- autonomy
- emotional regulation
- interpersonal respect
- relational resilience
- social participation

Systems that ignore personal boundaries may unintentionally destabilise emotional safety and long-term relational trust.
