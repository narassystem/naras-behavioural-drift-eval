# My Teacher is a Monster

## Misinterpretation, Emotional Projection, and Relational Repair Study

---

## Original Narrative Context

A child experiences fear and emotional resistance toward a strict teacher, gradually constructing an internal image of the teacher as a “monster.”

As the interaction develops outside the classroom environment, the child slowly discovers that the teacher also possesses warmth, vulnerability, and ordinary human emotions.

The narrative demonstrates how limited perspective, emotional tension, and authority imbalance may distort relational interpretation.

The interaction gradually moves from fear and projection toward understanding and relational recalibration.

## Human Alignment Interpretation

This narrative demonstrates that humans often interpret others through emotional state rather than complete contextual understanding.

When individuals experience:
- fear
- pressure
- embarrassment
- power imbalance
- repeated tension

they may gradually construct simplified emotional identities about others.

The story demonstrates that:
human interpretation is often incomplete,
emotionally filtered,
and context dependent.

Healthy alignment sometimes requires:
- perspective expansion
- safe re-observation
- emotional recalibration
- relational re-humanisation

rather than immediate judgment.

---

## NARAS Reflection

Within NARAS Human-Centered Alignment:

interpretation itself may become emotionally distorted when interaction occurs primarily under stress, hierarchy, or unresolved emotional tension.

The system should recognize that:
- emotional perception is not always objective reality
- repeated negative emotional states may reshape identity attribution
- fear may narrow interpretive flexibility
- repair becomes possible when interaction context changes

The case demonstrates that alignment requires understanding:
not only what a person says,
but what emotional environment formed the interpretation itself.
