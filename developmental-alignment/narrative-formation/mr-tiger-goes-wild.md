# Mr Tiger Goes Wild

## Identity Freedom and Social Conformity Negotiation Study

This narrative interaction study explores how individuality, behavioural restriction, authenticity, emotional suppression, and social conformity influence identity development and psychological self-regulation.

---

# Original Narrative Context

Mr Tiger lives within a highly civilized society governed by strict behavioural expectations, routines, and social norms.

Over time, he begins feeling emotionally constrained and disconnected from his natural instincts, eventually choosing to behave more freely and authentically.

Rather than framing wildness as purely rebellion or chaos, the narrative explores:
- identity tension
- emotional suppression
- authenticity seeking
- social adaptation
- balance between individuality and collective norms

The interaction structure supports reflective negotiation between personal freedom and social belonging.
