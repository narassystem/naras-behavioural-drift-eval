# Good News, Bad News

Cognitive Reframing and Perspective Flexibility Study

This narrative interaction study explores how interpretive framing, emotional flexibility, perspective shifting, and contextual reassessment influence meaning construction and emotional regulation.

---

## Original Narrative Context

A child encounters alternating positive and negative events within an emotionally contrasting narrative structure.

Rather than presenting fixed emotional conclusions, the story gradually reveals how the same situation may contain both disappointment and possibility depending on interpretation.

The interaction structure encourages emotional flexibility and multi-perspective reasoning.

## Narrative Interaction Design

The narrative structure supports interpretive flexibility through contrasting emotional framing.

Key interaction patterns include:

- alternating outcomes
- expectation shifts
- perspective reversal
- emotional contrast
- reflective questioning
- reinterpretation cycles
- uncertainty processing
- balanced emotional pacing

Rather than reinforcing rigid emotional reactions, the interaction design encourages flexible interpretation.

---

## Alignment Perspective

Human emotional experience is deeply influenced by interpretation rather than events alone.

Interpretive framing influences:

- emotional stability
- behavioural response
- resilience
- future expectations
- social understanding
- cognitive flexibility

Systems that influence interpretation therefore indirectly shape emotional trajectory.
