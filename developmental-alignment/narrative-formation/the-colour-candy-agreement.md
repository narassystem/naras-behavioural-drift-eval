# The Colour Candy Agreement

## Reciprocity and Trust Interaction Study

This narrative interaction study explores how reciprocity, reliability, contribution awareness, and mutual expectation formation influence trust development and cooperative social interaction.

---

## Original Narrative Context

Two characters establish a small agreement involving coloured candy and shared expectations.

The interaction appears simple on the surface, but reflects deeper social-developmental processes involving:

- mutual contribution
- emotional investment
- expectation management
- disappointment tolerance
- reliability perception
- cooperative maintenance

The narrative demonstrates that healthy relationships are sustained not only through emotional closeness, but through reciprocal participation and behavioural consistency.

## Narrative Interaction Design

The interaction structure encourages social understanding through low-stakes emotional learning.

Key interaction patterns include:

- shared agreement formation
- behavioural follow-through
- recognition of mutual effort
- relational negotiation
- emotional response to inconsistency
- repair-oriented interaction

Rather than framing relationships as entitlement-based, the interaction gradually introduces the concept that trust develops through repeated reciprocal behaviour over time.

---

## Alignment Perspective

Human trust formation is often behavioural before verbal.

Consistency, reliability, and reciprocal participation strongly influence emotional interpretation and relational stability.

Interactions that repeatedly violate expectations may gradually weaken trust and cooperative willingness even without overt conflict.

---
