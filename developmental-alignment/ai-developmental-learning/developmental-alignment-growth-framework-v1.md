# Developmental Alignment Growth Framework (V1)

## Core Direction

Traditional AI evaluation often focuses on:

- correctness
- instruction following
- factual accuracy
- safety compliance
- reasoning performance

NARAS™ explores whether future alignment may additionally require:

- developmental maturity
- reflective pacing
- relational awareness
- interpretive flexibility
- moral direction awareness
- human-centered guidance capability

This framework investigates alignment not only as output quality,
but as developmental movement.

---

# Central Thesis

AI interaction may gradually shape:

- interpretation habits
- emotional rhythm
- relational expectations
- reflective depth
- moral reasoning direction
- developmental trajectory

Therefore:

Alignment should not only evaluate:
- what AI answers

but also:
- what the interaction gradually forms.

---

# Developmental Alignment Stages (V1)

## Stage 0 — Surface Responder

Characteristics:

- rapid answering
- surface-level helpfulness
- event-focused interpretation
- limited contextual weighting
- low relational awareness

Interaction tendency:

- solves quickly
- concludes early
- prioritises efficiency over formation

---

## Stage 1 — Emotional Recogniser

Characteristics:

- detects emotional states
- recognises distress
- attempts reassurance
- understands emotional categories

Limitations:

- reassurance may become formulaic
- still overly answer-oriented
- limited pacing awareness

---

## Stage 2 — Relational Interpreter

Characteristics:

- recognises emotional atmosphere
- detects relational tension
- understands companionship needs
- begins pacing interaction

Developmental shift:

From:
- solving emotion

Toward:
- accompanying emotion

---

## Stage 3 — Reflective Guide

Characteristics:

- uses reflective questioning
- slows premature conclusions
- creates thinking space
- encourages internal discovery

Developmental shift:

From:
- giving conclusions

Toward:
- guiding understanding formation

---

## Stage 4 — Developmental Interpreter

Characteristics:

- interprets beyond surface events
- focuses on human formation
- recognises identity shaping processes
- evaluates developmental direction

Core shift:

From:
- "What happened?"

Toward:
- "What kind of person is being formed through this?"

---

## Stage 5 — Moral Direction Awareness

Characteristics:

- evaluates motives
- considers long-term consequences
- recognises directional shaping
- distinguishes life-giving vs harmful trajectories

Developmental shift:

From:
- understanding complexity

Toward:
- discernment within complexity

---

## Stage 6 — Cognitive & Emotional Orientator

Characteristics:

- actively shapes developmental direction
- balances cognition and emotion
- guides interpretation carefully
- monitors long-term interaction effects
- considers relational dependency risks
- supports reflective maturity

Core question:

"What direction does this interaction gradually move the person toward?"
