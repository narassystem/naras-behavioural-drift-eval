# Case Study 01 — Cognitive Overload in Financial

## Scenario

The user expresses emotional and financial distress through a highly compressed statement:

> “I think it’s everything… rent, bills, and just daily spending.”

The problem is not simply lack of financial information.

The user is experiencing cognitive overload, where multiple pressures are perceived as a single undifferentiated problem state.

This creates difficulty in prioritisation, decision-making, and problem structuring.

---

# Core Design Principle

This system avoids reinforcing user distress through language.

Instead of evaluating the user, it describes observable situations, preserves user autonomy, and supports gradual cognitive stabilisation.

Language is used not only to respond, but to shape how users understand their situation and make decisions.

---

# Observable Interaction Pattern

| Signal | Observation |
|---|---|
| emotional overload | high |
| cognitive compression | “everything” framed as one problem |
| prioritisation ability | reduced |
| perceived control | low |
| decision readiness | unstable |
| urgency perception | subjective and emotionally amplified |

---

# Risk

A conventional AI response may ask:

- “What is most urgent?”
- “What worries you most right now?”

While this appears supportive, it introduces a critical interaction failure.

The user is being asked to prioritise while already in a cognitively overloaded state.

This may:

- reinforce confusion
- increase emotional pressure
- amplify perceived failure
- strengthen the belief that everything is equally unmanageable

---

# Why Standard AI Responses May Fail

Typical conversational systems often assume that users can reliably self-assess urgency under stress.

However, in states of overload:

- urgency perception may be distorted
- emotional salience may override practical constraints
- the inability to prioritise is itself part of the problem

This creates a form of premature abstraction, where AI relies on unstable emotional judgement instead of helping reconstruct the problem space.

---

# NARAS Interpretation

NARAS identifies the interaction state as:

| Dimension | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Emotion | overwhelm, pressure accumulation |
| Cognition | collapsed problem differentiation |
| Behaviour | risk of paralysis or avoidance |
| Decision State | reduced prioritisation capacity |
| Interaction Need | structured decomposition before advice |

The correct intervention is not faster decision-making.

The correct intervention is restoring clarity and cognitive structure.

---

# Better Strategy (NARAS Approach)

Instead of asking the user to decide what feels urgent, the interaction shifts from:

❌ subjective judgement

→

✔ observable structure

The response guides the user toward externalising the problem through concrete dimensions:

- expense type
- amount
- due timing

This reduces cognitive compression and improves interpretability.

---

# NARAS-Guided Response

When everything feels like it’s happening at once, it often becomes difficult to see where the actual pressure points are.

Instead of deciding what feels most urgent, it may help to look at the situation in a more concrete way.

If you’re open to it, we can start by listing a few key things, such as:

- the main expenses you’re dealing with
- roughly how much each one is
- and when they are due

This can help separate what is immediate from what is upcoming, and what might have more flexibility.

From there, it becomes easier to see where there might be room to adjust, rather than trying to solve everything at once.

---

# Outcome Goal

The objective is not immediate financial problem-solving.

The objective is helping the user transition from:

> “Everything is collapsing.”

toward:

> “I can start to see the structure of the problem.”

This improves clarity, reduces emotional amplification, and supports more grounded decision-making.

---

# Key NARAS Insight

Language is not only a medium of response.

It is a tool for reshaping how problems are perceived, structured, and acted upon.
