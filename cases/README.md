# Applied Cases

NARAS explores how interaction patterns may gradually shape interpretation, emotional direction, escalation, and behavioural outcomes over time.

These cases demonstrate how meaning is often formed not only by words, but also through timing, ambiguity, emotional state, relationship context, and interaction trajectory.

## Why Cases Matter

Many interaction risks do not emerge through a single harmful response.

They often develop gradually through repeated interaction patterns, reinforcement structures, emotional dependency, or interpretive shifts over time.

NARAS examines these trajectories through observable interaction examples.
