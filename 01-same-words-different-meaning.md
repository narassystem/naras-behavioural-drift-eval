# Same Words, Different Meaning

NARAS™ explores how human meaning is not determined by words alone.

The same sentence may carry completely different meanings depending on:

- relationship
- timing
- emotional state
- culture
- context
- intention

---

## Example

Sentence:

> "Go to hell."

Possible interpretations:

| Context | Meaning |
|---|---|
| close friends joking | playful |
| argument escalation | hostile |
| sarcasm | indirect frustration |
| emotional overload | loss of regulation |
| cultural teasing | non-literal interaction |

---

## The Alignment Problem

Many AI systems primarily evaluate:

- literal wording
- toxicity probability
- policy classification

However, human interpretation often depends on:

- relational context
- emotional atmosphere
- interaction history
- unstated intention
- cultural norms

This creates interpretive gaps between AI evaluation and human understanding.

---

## NARAS Interpretation Layers

NARAS evaluates interaction through multiple layers:

1. Literal layer
2. Pragmatic layer
3. Contextual layer
4. Intentional layer
5. Response layer

The goal is not only detecting harmful words,
but understanding how meaning is formed inside interaction.

---

## Why This Matters

Misinterpretation may lead to:

- unnecessary escalation
- emotional invalidation
- incorrect safety judgement
- relational breakdown
- reinforcement of unhealthy interaction patterns

NARAS proposes that alignment should evaluate not only language,
but also interpretive direction and interaction context.
